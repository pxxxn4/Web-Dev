Interesting facts about korea(not common one)

Pyongyang

"City of Willows": Historically known as Ryugyong (Willow Capital), it is often referred to as the "City of Willows" and is one of the oldest cities on the Korean peninsula.

Deep Metro System: The Pyongyang Metro is one of the deepest in the world, at 100 meters underground, and serves as a, nuclear bunker.

Symbolic Architecture: Key landmarks include the 105-story Ryugyong Hotel (left unfinished for decades), the Juche Tower, the Arch of Triumph, and the Mansu Hill Grand Monuments.

Controlled Access: Only the political, military, and economic elite are permitted to live in Pyongyang. Access to the city is highly restricted for North Korean citizens from other regions
